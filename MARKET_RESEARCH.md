# Tatva Cafe — Pricing & Market Research

This document summarizes the market research and pricing methodology used to establish the Tatva Cafe menu pricing structure.

---

## Overview

Pricing was determined using a three-source methodology: cost analysis, competitor benchmarking, and customer survey data. The goal was to arrive at prices that are profitable, competitive within the St. Louis market, and perceived as fair by the target customer.

---

## Competitor Benchmarking

Eight vegetarian and South Indian restaurants in the greater St. Louis area were analyzed. Locations included Chesterfield, Creve Coeur, Maryland Heights, and St. Peters.

## Cost Analysis

Ingredient cost, preparation time, and a fixed overhead allocation were mapped to each menu item. The following table shows representative items.

| Item | Ingredient Cost | Prep Time (min) | Overhead Allocation | Total Cost | Price | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Plain Idli (4 pc) | $1.20 | 8 | $1.50 | $2.70 | $7.77 | 65% |
| Masala Dosa | $2.10 | 12 | $1.50 | $3.60 | $10.77 | 67% |
| Bobbatlu (Special) | $0.60 | 15 | $0.75 | $1.35 | $2.50 | 46% |
| Coconut Burfi | $0.35 | 10 | $0.75 | $1.10 | $1.47 | 25% |
| Mango Lassi | $0.90 | 3 | $0.75 | $1.65 | $5.77 | 71% |

Margins on sweets are lower, consistent with the volume-based strategy described above. Beverage margins are highest and support overall profitability.

## Customer Survey Results

27 survey responses were collected from early visitors and community members familiar with Indian cuisine in St. Louis.

**Q: How would you rate the value for money at Tatva Cafe?**

| Rating | Responses |
|---|---|
| Excellent | 14 (52%) |
| Good | 9 (33%) |
| Fair | 3 (11%) |
| Poor | 1 (4%) |

**Q: Compared to similar restaurants, Tatva's prices feel...**

| Response | Responses |
|---|---|
| Lower than expected | 11 (41%) |
| About right | 13 (48%) |
| Higher than expected | 3 (11%) |

**Q: How likely are you to return?**

| Response | Responses |
|---|---|
| Very likely | 19 (70%) |
| Likely | 6 (22%) |
| Unlikely | 2 (8%) |

**Key Insight:** 89% of respondents felt pricing was at or below expectations, and 92% indicated they would return. This validated the pricing model prior to the official menu launch.

---

## Conclusion

The final pricing structure reflects a deliberate positioning strategy: competitive on high-visibility entrees, volume-driven on sweets, and margin-optimized on beverages. All prices were set above the calculated cost floor and validated against both market benchmarks and customer perception data.
